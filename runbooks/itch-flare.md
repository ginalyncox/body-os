# Runbook: Itch Flare

**Trigger:** sudden itch on skin, often without visible cause. May be SFN-driven, allergen-driven (commercial yeast, certain proteins), or contact-residue (flour-water dried on skin).

## Triage (5 seconds)
- **Visible cause** (something just touched skin)? → Step 1.
- **No visible cause** (SFN-likely)? → Step 4.

## Steps
1. **Identify and remove the contact source.** Wash off the substance with cool, then warm soapy water. Cool first rinses; warm second cleans.
2. **Do not scratch.** Press firmly with a flat palm instead. Pressure satisfies the itch reflex without breaking skin or amplifying the signal.
3. **Cool damp cloth** on the area for 60–120 seconds. Not ice.
4. **If SFN-likely:** the itch is a misfiring nerve, not a real signal. Acknowledge it ("That's a phantom itch, my nerves are noisy today"), do not engage with it, redirect attention.
5. **Distraction over suppression.** Engage a different sensory channel — hold something textured, sip cool water, hum a low tone.
6. **Unscented lotion** if the skin tolerates it. Some flares are worsened by lotion — know your skin.

## Success criteria
- Itch intensity drops at least 2 points on the 1–10 scale within 5 minutes.
- No skin breakage from scratching.

## Log the flare
- Was there contact? With what?
- Is this a recurring pattern with that substance?
- If 3+ flares with the same suspected substance, move it to **Confirmed** in Known Triggers.

## Known confirmed triggers
- Commercial baker's yeast (active dry, instant) — do not contact bare skin.
- Dried flour-water paste — always rinse before it dries.

## Known cleared
- Mature wild sourdough starter on wet skin during stretch-and-folds.
