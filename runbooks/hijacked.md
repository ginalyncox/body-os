# Runbook: Hijacked

**Trigger:** sudden autonomic activation — racing heart, shallow breath, sweating, dread, feeling "taken over" by the body, sense of urgency with nothing to do.

## Acknowledge
Say out loud: *"My nervous system is firing. This is not danger. This is a wave."* Saying it out loud activates a different brain region than thinking it.

## Triage (10 seconds)
- Severity 1–4? → Run `scripts/reset-60s.md`.
- Severity 5–7? → Sit. Run `scripts/reset-5min.md`.
- Severity 8+? → Lie down. Run `scripts/reset-deep.md`.
- Cannot tell? → Treat as 7. Sit down now.

## Steps
1. **Sit or lie down.** Standing extends the spike. Always reduce postural load first.
2. **Cool-ish water on wrists or face.** Not ice (thermal shock). Cool tap water for 20 seconds.
3. **Physiological sigh × 6.** Double inhale through nose, long slow exhale through mouth. This is the fastest documented downregulator.
4. **Name what's around you.** 5 things you see, 4 you can touch, 3 you hear. Out loud.
5. **No decisions.** No texts, no posts, no Git commits, no calls. Decisions made hijacked are bad decisions.

## Success criteria
- Heart rate audibly slows.
- One full natural yawn or sigh (signs of vagal tone returning).
- Able to name the day of the week without effort.

## If not resolved in 15 minutes
- Drop to Red protocol.
- Lie down in a low-light room.
- One trusted person notified (no decisions about who — use the pre-written list in `infrastructure/people.md` once that file exists).
- Log the flare in Flare Log with what worked / didn't.

## Do NOT
- Push through to finish the task. The task is already lost. Finishing it makes the next spike worse.
- Caffeine.
- Doomscroll. Sensory input intensifies hijack.
- Self-criticize. The body had a reason. The postmortem finds it later, not now.
