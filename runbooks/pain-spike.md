# Runbook: Pain Spike

**Trigger:** sudden increase in burning, stabbing, or electrical pain, often in hands, feet, or face. Sometimes provoked by temperature, posture, or sleep debt; sometimes spontaneous.

## Triage (10 seconds)
- Severity 4–6? → Adjust posture, lukewarm water, redirect.
- Severity 7+? → Stop everything, lie down, run `scripts/reset-deep.md`, consider scheduled pain meds if in protocol.

## Steps
1. **Posture check.** Are you standing? Sit. Are you sitting tense? Recline. Pain extends with postural load.
2. **Temperature match.** Cool the part if it feels hot/burning; lukewarm-warm if it feels icy. Never extreme temps either direction.
3. **Compression test.** Light, even pressure (a soft sock over hand, a weighted blanket on legs) sometimes downregulates pain. Try — if it makes it worse, remove.
4. **Breath:** 4 in, 7 out. Long exhale specifically. Don't count to 4-7-8 — just longer out than in.
5. **Visualize the area as warm/neutral, not as the pain location.** Pain attention amplifies pain. This is not woo — it's documented descending modulation.
6. **Movement:** if pain is in feet/legs, very gentle ankle circles. If hands, very gentle finger fan. If face, slow jaw opens. Tiny movement, not stretching.

## Success criteria
- Pain drops at least 1 point within 10 minutes.
- If it doesn't, escalate to red protocol; this is not a willpower problem.

## Do NOT
- Ice directly on the painful area (thermal shock can intensify SFN pain).
- Push through to "finish" anything.
- Talk yourself out of it. The pain is real; the source is small-fiber dysfunction.

## Log
Every 7+ pain spike gets a Flare Log entry. Look for patterns weekly.
