# Runbook: Sensory Overload

**Trigger:** the world is suddenly too loud, too bright, too much. Often happens at the end of a long work block, in a store, or during multi-context days.

## Steps
1. **Reduce one input immediately.** Close eyes, OR cover ears, OR step out of the space. Don't try to reduce all at once — one channel.
2. **Find a low-load space.** Bathroom, bedroom, parked car, quiet hallway.
3. **Earplugs in OR hood/hat up OR sunglasses on.** Lowest-friction sensory subtraction tools.
4. **Sit. Eyes soft, not screwed shut.** Hard-closed eyes increase tension.
5. **One breath: 4 in, 8 out.** Long exhale.
6. **5 minutes minimum** before reentering. Even if you feel "fine" at 90 seconds.

## Success criteria
- Sound and light tolerance climbs back. You can open eyes in normal light without flinching.
- Brain feels like it's tracking one thread again.

## Prevention (the real fix)
- Limit context-switching on yellow days.
- Earplugs in pocket as default carry.
- Sunglasses indoors are fine. They are not weird; they are tools.
- One screen at a time when symptomatic.

## Note
Sensory overload is often the first signal of an oncoming autonomic spike or pain spike. Treat it as a warning, not just a moment. Drop a tier preemptively if it happens twice in a day.
