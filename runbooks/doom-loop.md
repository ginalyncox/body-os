# Runbook: Doom Loop

**Trigger:** thought spiral. "It won't get better." "I'm broken." "Nothing I do works." "I'm a burden." The thoughts feel true and urgent. They are neither.

## Recognize
Doom loops have a signature: the content feels new but the *shape* is familiar. If you've thought this exact thing before, even with different words, it's a loop. Loops are tractable.

## Steps
1. **Name it out loud.** *"I am in a doom loop. The thoughts are real but they are not accurate right now."*
2. **Body check first, thoughts second.** When did you last eat? Drink water? Sleep? Pee? 90% of doom loops have a physical substrate. Address the body before debating the thoughts.
3. **Take care of one body need.** Water, food, bathroom, blanket, light off. The smallest one available.
4. **Change the input channel.** Doom loops live on screens, in silence, in bed. Move to a different room, put on background sound, open a window.
5. **Do not problem-solve the thoughts.** Loops are not arguments. You will not win by being smarter than them. You win by waiting them out from a regulated body.
6. **Talk to a human if safe to do so** — not to be talked out of it, just to be witnessed.
7. **988 (call or text)** if anything tips toward not wanting to be here. No question, no hesitation. This is what 988 is for.

## Success criteria
- Thoughts soften from absolute ("never," "always," "nothing") to relative.
- A small body need has been met.
- You can name one true thing that's not catastrophic. ("The starter is alive." "I have a saddle stool." "Tomorrow is a different day.")

## Do NOT
- Engage with the content of the thoughts as if they're a debate to be won.
- Make any decision about work, money, relationships, or your future while in a loop.
- Self-criticize for being in the loop. Loops are not character defects; they're nervous system patterns.
