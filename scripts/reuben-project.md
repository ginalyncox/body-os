# 🥪 The Reuben Project

**For the husband who said "smells like toast."**
**Marble rye bread (yours) + deli ingredients (his) = a Reuben at home.**

You bake. He eats. You eat your own bread on the side, the way your body actually likes it. Everyone wins.

---

## ⏱ 1-Minute Reset (before you start)

1. Sit. Feet supported.
2. Hands palm-down on thighs.
3. **Breath:** double inhale through nose, long exhale through mouth. **×4**
4. **Say out loud:** *"I'm being gentle with this. The dough is patient."*
5. Sip water.

→ Now you can bake.

---

## 📅 Project Timeline

This is a 2-day project that uses a Saturday-Sunday or Sunday-Monday rhythm.

- **Day 0 (evening):** Feed starter.
- **Day 1 (morning):** Mix doughs. Bulk ferment. Shape and combine.
- **Day 1 (evening):** Cold retard begins.
- **Day 2 (morning or afternoon):** Bake. Cool fully (60+ min).
- **Day 2 or 3 (any time):** Make Russian dressing. Assemble and griddle the Reuben.

**Save for a green or yellow-good day. Don't start what you can't finish.**

## 🍞 PART 1: The Marble Rye

### Day 0 (Evening) — Feed Starter

1. Stir starter with spatula. Discard to ~50g.
2. Add **50g bread flour + 50g water**.
3. Stir. Loose lid. Back in 78°F box.
4. **Wait 4–6 hours** before mixing doughs.

### Day 1 — Mix Both Doughs (10 min)

You're making two doughs at the same time. Mix them in parallel, in two separate bowls.

**LIGHT DOUGH (bowl 1):**

| Item | Amount |
| :--- | ---: |
| Bread flour | **250 g** |
| Rye flour | **50 g** |
| Lukewarm water | **210 g** |
| Active starter | **60 g** |
| Molasses | **15 g** |
| Salt | **6 g** |
| Caraway seeds (optional) | **5 g** |

**DARK DOUGH (bowl 2):**

| Item | Amount |
| :--- | ---: |
| Bread flour | **200 g** |
| Rye flour | **100 g** |
| Lukewarm water | **210 g** |
| Active starter | **60 g** |
| Molasses | **15 g** |
| Cocoa powder | **15 g** |
| Instant coffee | **5 g** |
| Salt | **6 g** |
| Caraway seeds (optional) | **5 g** |

For each bowl, in order:
1. Water + starter + molasses. Swirl with wooden spoon.
2. Add cocoa + coffee (dark bowl only). Swirl.
3. Add flours, salt, and seeds.
4. Stir until shaggy.
5. Cover. Rest 30 min.

### Day 1 — Bulk Ferment (4–5 hours, parallel)

Same routine, both bowls:
- 3 stretch-and-folds per dough, every 30–45 min
- Wet both hands, fold edge to center, quarter turn, repeat ×4
- Rinse hands immediately after each round
- Cover and rest between rounds

Doughs are ready when:
- Each has risen ~50%
- Visible bubbles on surface and sides
- Jiggly when bowl is nudged

### Day 1 — Shape the Marble (10 min)

1. Lightly flour counter (pour, never scoop).
2. Wet hands. Turn **light dough** out. Pat into a rectangle, roughly 8×10".
3. Turn **dark dough** out. Pat into a rectangle of the same size.
4. **Stack dark on top of light.** Press gently to bond — don't squish out the gas.
5. Starting from a short edge, **roll up tightly like a jelly roll.**
6. Pinch the seam to seal. Tuck the ends under.
7. **Place seam-down in a buttered or parchment-lined loaf pan.**

### Day 1 — Cold Retard (10–14 hours)

Cover the loaf pan with plastic wrap or a damp towel. Into the fridge.

### Day 2 — Bake

1. **Preheat oven to 425°F.** No dutch oven needed for pan loaf.
2. **Pull loaf pan from fridge while oven preheats** (~30 min on the counter).
3. *(Optional: brush top with water for a shinier crust.)*
4. **425°F · 35–40 min** until internal temp hits **200°F** at the center.
5. Tip the loaf out of the pan onto a cooling rack.
6. **Cool 60 minutes minimum** before slicing. Ideally cool fully — rye benefits from a longer rest, often overnight.

### Slicing

- Sharp serrated knife
- **3/8" slices** (slightly thicker than typical sandwich bread)
- The swirl should reveal itself in spirals or stripes

## 🥫 PART 2: The Russian Dressing (5 min)

Mix in a small bowl:

- **1/2 cup mayonnaise**
- **2 Tbsp ketchup**
- **1 Tbsp prepared horseradish** (more if he likes heat)
- **1 Tbsp sweet pickle relish**
- **1 tsp Worcestershire sauce** (skip if MCAS-flaring)
- **1/2 tsp paprika**
- **Pinch of salt and pepper**
- *(Optional: 1 tsp minced onion, splash of lemon juice)*

Stir. Cover. **Refrigerate at least 1 hour before using** — flavors meld dramatically.

Yields enough for 4–6 Reubens. Keeps a week in the fridge.

---

## 🥪 PART 3: Assemble & Griddle (15 min)

### Per sandwich:

- 2 slices marble rye (3/8" thick)
- 5–6 oz corned beef (mounded — more than seems reasonable)
- 2 slices Swiss (1.5 oz total)
- 1/4 cup sauerkraut (**drained and squeezed dry** — wet kraut = soggy sandwich)
- Russian dressing (generous)
- Butter (for griddling)

### Build, bottom to top:

1. **Butter the outside** of both bread slices (the sides that touch the pan).
2. **Russian dressing** on the inside of both slices.
3. Bottom slice → **Swiss cheese**
4. → **Corned beef** (mounded)
5. → **Sauerkraut** (dry, between the cheeses)
6. → **Swiss cheese** (second slice)
7. → Top slice, buttered side facing out.

### Griddle (cast iron, the best way):

1. Cast iron skillet over **medium-low** heat. Preheat 3 min.
2. Sandwich in the pan.
3. **Press down** with a spatula or another small pan.
4. **4–5 min per side**, pressing intermittently.
5. Done when bread is deeply golden, cheese fully melted, filling hot through.
6. *(Resist cranking the heat — too hot = burnt bread before melted cheese.)*

**Or panini press:** preheat, sandwich in, close lid, 5–6 min. Done.

## ⚠️ MCAS Notes

**Skip caraway** if you've reacted to seeds before, or if it's a luteal-phase / yellow-day. The bread is still excellent without it.

**Skip Worcestershire** in the Russian dressing if MCAS-flaring. The dressing still works.

**For the husband's Reuben — no modifications needed.** This is his sandwich.

**For your slice — no Reuben fillings.** Just bread + olive oil + egg + herbs.

---

## 🟢🟡🔴 Day Check Before You Start

- **Green day?** Full project. Both doughs, all components.
- **Yellow day?** Make one dough (skip the marble — just a single rye loaf), buy pre-made Russian dressing, simpler version.
- **Red day?** Save for next week. The starter is fine. The corned beef freezes. The Reuben is patient.

---

## ⚠️ Skin Safety Rules

- **Wet hand first, every time.**
- **Rinse immediately** after dough contact.
- **Pour flour** — never scoop with bare hands.
- **Lukewarm water** — never cold tap, never hot.

---

## 💡 First-Time Tips

- **Make the bread the day before** the Reuben. Marble rye is significantly better after 12+ hours of rest. Try to bake on Saturday for a Sunday lunch.
- **Buy the corned beef the day you'll eat it**, not days in advance — fresh from the deli is much better than refrigerated for 3 days.
- **Squeeze the sauerkraut hard** before assembly. Wet kraut destroys the sandwich.
- **The first sandwich is the practice run.** It'll be good. The second one (using what you learned) will be the great one.
