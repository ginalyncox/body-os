# Script: shutdown

*End-of-day script, run regardless of what got done. The system needs a clean shutdown sequence.*

## Trigger condition
- Within 30 minutes of intended bedtime
- After any black or red day, no exceptions
- Anytime you find yourself working past midnight and want to stop but can't

## Preconditions
- Sit on the saddle stool, the bed, or wherever sleep will happen
- Lights dim
- Hands washed if dough or food was involved

## Steps (~10 minutes)
1. **Close open loops fast.** Save any work. Close the laptop lid. Don't tidy mentally — just save and close.
2. **Phone: airplane mode or Do Not Disturb.** Charging across the room, not the bed.
3. **Water by the bed.** Lukewarm, not cold.
4. **Teeth.** Even a quick brush.
5. **One sentence out loud about today.** Not an evaluation, just a fact. *"Today I fed the starter." "Today I rested." "Today I existed."* Any of those is enough.
6. **One sentence about tomorrow's first move.** Not a plan, a single step. *"Tomorrow I drink water when I wake up."*
7. **Lights off.**
8. **Reset-deep breathing pattern (4 in, 8 out) for 5 minutes** while horizontal.

## Success criteria
- The day is over. There is nothing more being asked of you.
- The body knows it is allowed to sleep.

## The point
A shutdown script makes the body trust that the day actually ends. Without it, the nervous system stays half-on overnight, and the next day starts at a worse baseline.
