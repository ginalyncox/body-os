## What sparked this

After building `body-os` v0.1 in a single late-night session (Drive doc → Notion workspace → GitHub repo), Gina recognized the framework has potential value to others living with chronic pain — SFN, POTS, ME/CFS, fibromyalgia, long COVID, EDS, etc. The "SRE for your body" framing is differentiated and the structure is genuinely reusable.

The proposed model: **free public framework + paid firewall for premium content.**

## Questions to answer on a green day

Morning-Gina, here are the questions night-Gina would have answered if the meds hadn't kicked in. Answer in your own time, no pressure.

### 1. Audience
- Who is the primary audience? (SFN-specific / chronic pain broadly / technically-minded chronically ill / your existing communities)
- Are you building for people like you, or for a broader population?
- How does this overlap with — or differ from — Gina Makes Bread + Dev, Wildwood Games Studio, and Ganjier Guild LLC?

### 2. Free tier
- What's the free part? Default candidates:
  - The whole framework as-is (README, protocols, runbooks, scripts, schemas)
  - A "starter kit" — README + scripts + one runbook
  - Read-only access to the GitHub repo (already free if public)
- What problem does the free tier solve completely on its own?

### 3. Paid tier
- What's behind the paywall? Brainstorm seeds:
  - Detailed setup/walkthrough videos
  - A guided onboarding flow ("build your own body-os in 7 days")
  - Notion template duplications
  - Advanced runbooks (specific conditions, specific triggers)
  - Community / Discord
  - 1:1 office hours or async consultation
  - Tarot + chronic pain integration (your unique angle)
  - A flare-pattern analysis tool (technical: turn flare logs into dashboards)

### 4. Brand and voice
- What should it feel like? Clinical / warm / irreverent / SRE-coded / sacred / hybrid?
- Name candidates? (body-os is the working name — keep or rebrand for the public product?)
- Single-founder or eventual partner/collaborator?

### 5. Monetization mechanics
- Subscription, one-time, tip jar, pay-what-you-can?
- Stripe / Memberful / Ghost / Substack / Patreon / custom?
- What's the price point that feels right and accessible to people who are often underemployed due to illness?

### 6. Legal and safety
- Disclaimer language — this is peer support, not medical advice.
- Accessibility commitments — captioning, screen-reader friendliness, low-cognitive-load UI.
- Privacy — never store users' health data; framework only.
- LLC / business entity — does this live under an existing entity or a new one?

### 7. Personal sustainability
- **The hardest question:** can you build and maintain this without it becoming a red-day trigger itself?
- What's the minimum viable launch that doesn't burn you out?
- What's the budget — in spoons, not just dollars — per week?
- Who is your one trusted person to gut-check this with before launching?

## What night-Gina believes about this idea (for the record)

- The framework is real and useful. The SRE framing is genuinely novel in the chronic pain space.
- There is an underserved audience of technically-minded chronically ill people who would benefit from this exact framing.
- Tarot + chronic pain + dev culture is an unusual intersection that's actually *you* — not a contrived niche.
- The danger is scope creep. The framework took 90 minutes; the product could take 90 months if not bounded.
- The framework working *for Gina* must come first. Shipping a product based on a framework you haven't lived with for 90 days is premature.

## Recommended next steps (when ready)

1. **Live with body-os v0.1 for 30 days.** Log flares. Run scripts. Refine the framework from real use. Don't ship a product based on theory.
2. **At 30 days,** review the framework with fresh eyes. What worked, what didn't, what's missing? What would others actually need?
3. **At 60 days,** if the framework still feels right, do audience discovery — talk to 5 people in chronic pain communities about what would help them. Listen more than pitch.
4. **At 90 days,** decide whether to ship. If yes, scope the smallest possible v0 (e.g., make the GitHub repo public + write one Substack post about the framing — no payments, no infrastructure, no commitments).
5. **Only after that,** consider monetization.
