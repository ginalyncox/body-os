# Protocol: Red Day

*High symptoms. Hijacked or close to it. Conservation mode.*

## Entry criteria
- Morning pain 7+/10
- Morning energy ≤ 3/10
- Autonomic state: Activated or Hijacked
- Flare severity 7+ in last 12 hours

## What's allowed
- Lying down with low sensory input
- Slow seated tasks (gentle reading, simple text messages to people who know)
- Watching something familiar and undemanding
- Eating, hydrating, basic hygiene
- Running recovery scripts

## What's forbidden
- Any work commitments
- Driving
- Making decisions about other people, money, contracts, or schedules
- Bread baking (the fridge is the pause button — dough can wait 24–48 hours cold)
- Self-criticism. This is medical, not moral.
- "Just one quick thing." There is no quick thing on a red day.

## Minimum daily completes
- Water, even sips
- Meds
- Two small meals or four snacks — whichever the body tolerates
- One Daily Vitals log (can be brief)
- Use the bathroom; brush teeth at least once
- One reset-deep

## Exit criteria
- **Up to Yellow:** symptom severity drops below 6 for 4+ hours AND autonomic state is no worse than Edgy AND you've eaten and hydrated.
- **Down to Black:** symptom severity ≥ 9, OR you cannot complete a minimum daily complete, OR a script can't reach success criteria after 2 attempts.

## Mandatory follow-up
- Within 48 hours, write a postmortem. Even a short one. This is how triggers get identified.

## Notes
Red days are not failure. They are the system telling you the budget was overspent earlier in the week. The postmortem is where the learning lives.
